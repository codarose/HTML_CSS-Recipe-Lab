Veggie Springrolls


Springrolls are one of my favorite Vietnamese food because they are very simple to make, super delicious and by changing the dipping sauce you've got yourself almost an entirely different dish. This recipe is a probably one of the simplist version one could make. Try it and let me know what you think!

Ingredients:

  • 1 Package of Rice Paper
  • 1 Bundle of Rice Noodles
  • 3 Eggs
  • 1 Cucumber
  • 1 Head of lettuce
  • 1 Carrot
  • 1 Small Bag of Mix Asian Mints
  • 1 Cup of Hoisin Sauce
  • 1 Cup of Water
  • 1 Table Spoon of Sugar
  • 1 Clove of Garlic
  • 1/2 Lime
  • 1 Table Spoon Crushed Peanuts (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon of Salt

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice noodles in a pot of water until the noodles are soft at a pinch. Then rinse the noodles in cold water and plate it.
  2. Beat the eggs with 1 tsp of salt. Then cook in a flat pan until golden yellow and soft-hard texture. Transfer to cutting plate and cut into .5 inch thick strips.
  3. Cut the cucumber, mix asian mints, and carrots into thin strips approximately 1/8 inch thick.
  4. Clean the lettuce and separate and stack the leafs individually on a plate.
  5. Mix the hoisin sauce, sugar, diced garlic, and lime into a pot until boiling. Pour the finish product into a small dipping bowl and sprinkle peanuts on top.
  6. Have a large bowl of warm water. Quickly dip the rice paper into it and put it flat on a large plate. Place 1 lettuce leaf on top, closest to you. stack on the eggs and a pinch of veggies, and noodles on top of each other. Starting from the end nearest you, roll the paper forward one round. Stop & hold that position tight! Then fold in the right & left sides. Now continue to roll forward until the wrap is complete.
  7. Dip your roll into the sauce and enjoy! You may add Siracha or other hot sauce into your sauce for an enhance experience.
  8. Enjoy!
